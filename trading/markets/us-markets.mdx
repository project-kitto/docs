## Trading Mechanics

### Settlement & Clearing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trade Execution">
    Order executed on exchange at agreed price
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trade Date (T)">
    Day the transaction occurs
  </Step>
  <Step title="Settlement (T+2)">
    Cash and securities change hands 2 business days later
  </Step>
  <Step title="Good Faith Violation">
    Selling stock before paying for it can result in account restrictions
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Pattern Day Trading Rules

<Warning>
  **PDT Rule**: Accounts under $25,000 are limited to 3 day trades in 5 business days.
</Warning>

<Accordion title="Day Trading Requirements">
  **Definition**: Buying and selling the same security on the same day

  **Requirements for PDT accounts**:
  - Maintain minimum $25,000 equity
  - Use margin account (not cash account)
  - 4:1 intraday buying power

  **Consequences of violations**:
  - Account flagged as PDT
  - 90-day restriction on day trading
  - Must meet minimum equity requirement
</Accordion>

## Tax Implications

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Capital Gains Tax">
    <div className="space-y-4">
      **Short-term (< 1 year)**:
      - Taxed as ordinary income
      - Rates: 10% to 37% (2024)

      **Long-term (≥ 1 year)**:
      - Preferential tax rates
      - 0%, 15%, or 20% based on income

      ```javascript
      // 2024 Long-term Capital Gains Rates
      const capitalGainsRates = {
        single: {
          '0%': 'Up to $47,025',
          '15%': '$47,026 to $518,900',
          '20%': 'Over $518,900'
        },
        marriedJoint: {
          '0%': 'Up to $94,050',
          '15%': '$94,051 to $583,750',
          '20%': 'Over $583,750'
        }
      };
      ```
    </div>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Dividend Taxation">
    <div className="space-y-4">
      **Qualified Dividends**:
      - Taxed at capital gains rates (0%, 15%, 20%)
      - Must hold stock for required period

      **Non-Qualified Dividends**:
      - Taxed as ordinary income
      - REITs, some foreign companies

      <Tip>
        Hold dividend stocks in tax-advantaged accounts (IRA, 401k) when possible to defer taxes.
      </Tip>
    </div>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Tax-Loss Harvesting">
    <div className="space-y-4">
      **Strategy**: Sell losing positions to offset gains

      **Wash Sale Rule**:
      - Cannot buy same/similar security 30 days before or after sale
      - Loss disallowed if violated
      - Holding period and cost basis adjusted

      **Benefits**:
      - Reduce current year tax liability
      - Carry forward unused losses
      - Up to $3,000 annual ordinary income offset
    </div>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Popular Investment Vehicles

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Individual Stocks" icon="building">
    **Direct company ownership**

    **Pros**: Full control, unlimited upside
    **Cons**: Higher risk, requires research

    **Best for**: Active investors with time for analysis
  </Card>

  <Card title="ETFs" icon="chart-pie">
    **Diversified, low-cost funds**

    **Pros**: Instant diversification, low fees
    **Cons**: Market returns only, no outperformance

    **Popular**: SPY, QQQ, VTI, SCHB
  </Card>

  <Card title="Mutual Funds" icon="users">
    **Professionally managed funds**

    **Pros**: Professional management, research
    **Cons**: Higher fees, less flexibility

    **Types**: Index funds, active funds, target-date
  </Card>

  <Card title="Options" icon="arrows-split-up-and-left">
    **Derivatives for hedging/speculation**

    **Pros**: Leverage, hedging capabilities
    **Cons**: Complex, time decay, high risk

    **Requires**: Advanced knowledge and approval

## Getting Started Checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a Broker">
    **Popular Options**:
    - **Charles Schwab**: Full-service, excellent research
    - **Fidelity**: Low costs, great customer service
    - **Interactive Brokers**: Advanced tools, global access
    - **TD Ameritrade**: Comprehensive platform (now Schwab)
    - **E*TRADE**: User-friendly, good for beginners
  </Step>

  <Step title="Account Types">
    **Taxable Account**: Full flexibility, immediate access
    **IRA**: Tax advantages, retirement focus
    **401(k)**: Employer match, higher limits
    **Roth IRA**: Tax-free growth, flexible withdrawals
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start Small">
    - Begin with broad market ETFs (SPY, VTI)
    - Gradually add individual stocks
    - Never invest more than you can afford to lose
    - Consider dollar-cost averaging strategy
  </Step>

  <Step title="Continue Learning">
    - Follow earnings reports and company news
    - Read annual reports (10-K) and quarterly reports (10-Q)
    - Track your investments and learn from results
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **For Beginners**: Start with low-cost index funds (VTI, VXUS) to get market exposure while you learn individual stock analysis.
</Tip>

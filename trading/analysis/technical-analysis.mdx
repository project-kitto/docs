### Moving Averages: The Foundation of Trend Analysis

<div className="space-y-6">
  <div className="bg-slate-900 p-6 rounded-xl border border-slate-800">
    <h4 className="text-slate-100 font-bold text-lg mb-4">Simple Moving Average (SMA)</h4>

    <div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">
      <div>
        <h5 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-3">What It Does</h5>
        <p className="text-slate-300 text-sm mb-4">
          Calculates the average closing price over a specific number of periods. It "smooths out" price fluctuations to reveal the underlying trend direction.
        </p>

        <div className="bg-slate-800 p-4 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
          <h6 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-2">Formula</h6>
          <code className="text-slate-400 text-sm">
            SMA = (Sum of closing prices over N periods) ÷ N
          </code>
          <p className="text-slate-400 text-xs mt-2">
            Example: 20-day SMA = (Day 1 close + Day 2 close + ... + Day 20 close) ÷ 20
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div>
        <h5 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-3">Common Periods & Uses</h5>
        <div className="space-y-3">
          <div className="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
            <div className="flex justify-between items-center mb-1">
              <span className="text-slate-200 font-medium">20-Day SMA</span>
              <span className="text-slate-400 text-xs">Short-term</span>
            </div>
            <p className="text-slate-400 text-xs">Day trading, short-term trends</p>
          </div>

          <div className="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
            <div className="flex justify-between items-center mb-1">
              <span className="text-slate-200 font-medium">50-Day SMA</span>
              <span className="text-slate-400 text-xs">Medium-term</span>
            </div>
            <p className="text-slate-400 text-xs">Swing trading, intermediate trends</p>
          </div>

          <div className="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
            <div className="flex justify-between items-center mb-1">
              <span className="text-slate-200 font-medium">200-Day SMA</span>
              <span className="text-slate-400 text-xs">Long-term</span>
            </div>

## Support & Resistance: The Foundation of Price Action

<div className="bg-slate-900 p-6 rounded-xl border border-slate-800 mb-8">
  <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-slate-100 mb-4">Understanding Market Floors and Ceilings</h3>
  <p className="text-slate-300 mb-4">
    Support and resistance are the most fundamental concepts in technical analysis. They represent psychological price levels where buying or selling pressure has historically been strong enough to stop or reverse price movements.
  </p>

  <div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">
    <div className="bg-slate-800 p-4 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
      <h4 className="text-green-400 font-semibold mb-2 flex items-center gap-2">
        <Icon icon="arrow-up" size={16} />
        Support Level
      </h4>
      <p className="text-slate-400 text-sm">
        A price level where buying interest is strong enough to overcome selling pressure. Think of it as a "floor" that price bounces off of.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div className="bg-slate-800 p-4 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
      <h4 className="text-red-400 font-semibold mb-2 flex items-center gap-2">
        <Icon icon="arrow-down" size={16} />
        Resistance Level
      </h4>
      <p className="text-slate-400 text-sm">
        A price level where selling interest is strong enough to overcome buying pressure. Think of it as a "ceiling" that price gets rejected from.
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div className="space-y-8">
  <div className="bg-slate-900 p-6 rounded-xl border border-slate-800">
    <h4 className="text-slate-100 font-bold text-lg mb-4">How to Identify Support & Resistance</h4>

    <div className="space-y-4">
      <div className="bg-slate-800 p-4 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
        <h5 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-3">Step 1: Look for Multiple Touches</h5>
        <p className="text-slate-400 text-sm mb-3">
          The more times a price level is tested without being broken, the stronger it becomes. Look for at least 2-3 touches to confirm a level.
        </p>
        <div className="bg-slate-700 p-3 rounded-lg">
          <p className="text-xs text-slate-500">
            <strong>Example:</strong> If Apple stock bounced off $150 three times over two months, $150 becomes a strong support level.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div className="bg-slate-800 p-4 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
        <h5 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-3">Step 2: Draw Horizontal Lines</h5>
        <p className="text-slate-400 text-sm mb-3">
          Connect the highs (for resistance) or lows (for support) that occurred at similar price levels. Don't worry about being exact—zones are more important than precise lines.
        </p>
        <div className="bg-slate-700 p-3 rounded-lg">
          <p className="text-xs text-slate-500">
            <strong>Tip:</strong> Support and resistance are zones, not exact prices. A level at $100 might actually hold between $99.50-$100.50.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div className="bg-slate-800 p-4 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
        <h5 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-3">Step 3: Watch for Volume Confirmation</h5>
        <p className="text-slate-400 text-sm mb-3">
          Strong support/resistance levels often coincide with high volume. When price bounces off a level with heavy volume, it confirms the level's importance.
        </p>
      </div>

      <div className="bg-slate-800 p-4 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
        <h5 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-3">Step 4: Monitor for Breaks</h5>
        <p className="text-slate-400 text-sm mb-3">
          When a level is broken with significant volume, it often signals a new trend direction. The broken level then switches roles.
        </p>
        <div className="bg-slate-700 p-3 rounded-lg">
          <p className="text-xs text-slate-500">
            <strong>Role Reversal:</strong> Broken support becomes resistance, and broken resistance becomes support.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="bg-slate-900 p-6 rounded-xl border border-slate-800">
    <h4 className="text-slate-100 font-bold text-lg mb-4">Types of Support & Resistance</h4>

    <div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6">
      <div>
        <h5 className="text-slate-200 font-semibold mb-4">Psychological Levels</h5>
        <div className="space-y-3">
          <div className="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
            <h6 className="text-slate-200 font-medium">Round Numbers</h6>
            <p className="text-slate-400 text-sm">$50, $100, $1000 - humans naturally gravitate toward round numbers</p>
          </div>

          </div>
          <div className="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
            <h6 className="text-slate-200 font-medium">Previous Breakout Points</h6>
            <p className="text-slate-400 text-sim">Where major moves began or ended</p>

        </div>

## Common Beginner Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

<div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 my-8">

    </div>
  </div>

  <div className="bg-slate-900 p-6 rounded-xl border border-slate-800">
    <div className="flex items-center gap-3 mb-4">

      </div>
      <h4 className="font-bold text-slate-100">Ignoring Volume</h4>
    </div>
    <div className="text-sm text-slate-300 mb-3">

    </div>
    <div className="bg-slate-800 p-3 rounded-lg border border-slate-700">
      <h5 className="text-green-400 font-medium mb-1">Solution</h5>
      <p className="text-slate-400 text-xs">
        Always check volume. High volume breakouts are more reliable than low volume ones. Volume should increase in the direction of the trend.

  <div className="bg-slate-900 p-6 rounded-xl border border-slate-800">

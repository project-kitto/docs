## Key Technical Indicators

### Moving Averages

<Accordion title="Simple Moving Average (SMA)">
  **Formula**: Sum of closing prices √∑ Number of periods

  ```python
  # 20-day Simple Moving Average
  SMA_20 = (Close_1 + Close_2 + ... + Close_20) / 20

  # Common periods:
  # Short-term: 10, 20 days
  # Medium-term: 50 days
  # Long-term: 100, 200 days
  ```

  **Trading Signals:**
  - Price above SMA = Bullish trend
  - Price below SMA = Bearish trend
  - SMA crossovers indicate trend changes
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Exponential Moving Average (EMA)">
  **Formula**: More weight given to recent prices

  ```python
  # EMA gives more weight to recent prices
  # Reacts faster to price changes than SMA

  EMA_today = (Close_today √ó Multiplier) + (EMA_yesterday √ó (1 - Multiplier))
  Multiplier = 2 / (Period + 1)
  ```

  **Advantages:**
  - Faster response to price changes
  - Better for short-term trading
  - Reduces lag compared to SMA
</Accordion>

## Support & Resistance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify Key Levels">
    Look for areas where price repeatedly bounced or stalled
  </Step>
  <Step title="Draw Horizontal Lines">
    Connect multiple touches at similar price levels
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch for Breaks">
    Significant volume on break confirms new direction
  </Step>
  <Step title="Role Reversal">
    Broken support becomes resistance, broken resistance becomes support
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Pro Tip**: The more times a level is tested, the more significant it becomes. Round numbers (like $50, $100) often act as psychological support/resistance.
</Tip>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

  <Card title="Over-Analysis" icon="exclamation-triangle" color="#6b7280">

  </Card>

    Volume confirms price movements. Low volume breakouts often fail.

## Portfolio Rebalancing Strategies

<Steps>
  <Step title="📅 Calendar Rebalancing" icon="calendar">
    <div className="bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200 p-4 rounded-lg">
      <h4 className="font-semibold text-gray-800">Time-Based Approach</h4>
      <p className="text-gray-700 mt-2">Rebalance portfolio at fixed intervals regardless of market movements.</p>
      <div className="mt-3 space-y-2 text-sm">
        <div><strong>Quarterly:</strong> For active investors with high conviction</div>
        <div><strong>Semi-Annual:</strong> Most common approach for retail investors</div>
        <div><strong>Annual:</strong> Suitable for long-term buy-and-hold investors</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </Step>

  <Step title=" Threshold Rebalancing" icon="bullseye">
    <div className="bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200 p-4 rounded-lg">
      <h4 className="font-semibold text-gray-800">Deviation-Based Approach</h4>
      <p className="text-gray-700 mt-2">Rebalance when asset allocation drifts beyond predetermined thresholds.</p>
      <div className="mt-3 space-y-2 text-sm">
        <div><strong>5% Rule:</strong> Rebalance when any asset class deviates by 5%</div>
        <div><strong>Example:</strong> 60/40 stocks/bonds → Rebalance if becomes 65/35 or 55/45</div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </Step>

  <Step title=" Hybrid Approach" icon="arrows-rotate">
    <div className="bg-gray-50 border border-gray-200 p-4 rounded-lg">
      <h4 className="font-semibold text-gray-800">Combined Strategy</h4>
      <p className="text-gray-700 mt-2">Check quarterly, rebalance only if thresholds are breached.</p>
      <div className="mt-3 text-sm">
        Provides discipline while avoiding unnecessary transaction costs.
      </div>
    </div>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Dollar-Cost Averaging vs Lump Sum

<div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-6 my-8">
  <div className="border-2 border-gray-200 rounded-lg p-6">
    <h3 className="text-xl font-bold text-gray-700 mb-4"> Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)</h3>

    **How it works:**
    <div className="text-sm space-y-2 mb-4">
      <div>• Invest fixed amount regularly (e.g., $500/month)</div>
      <div>• Buy more shares when prices are low</div>
      <div>• Buy fewer shares when prices are high</div>
      <div>• Reduces impact of market volatility</div>
    </div>

    **Example: $12,000 over 12 months**

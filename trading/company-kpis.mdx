## Understanding Financial Metrics

Financial metrics are quantitative measures used to evaluate a company's performance, financial health, and investment potential. These key performance indicators (KPIs) help investors make informed decisions by providing objective data about a company's operations and market position.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Valuation Metrics" icon="dollar-sign" color="#6b7280">
    **Purpose**: Determine if a stock is fairly priced relative to its fundamentals

    **Key Insight**: Compare current market price to company's intrinsic value based on earnings, sales, book value, and growth prospects. Essential for identifying undervalued or overvalued opportunities.

    **Common Ratios**: P/E, P/S, P/B, PEG, EV/EBITDA
  </Card>
  <Card title="Profitability Metrics" icon="trending-up" color="#6b7280">
    **Purpose**: Measure how efficiently a company converts revenue into profit

    **Key Insight**: Analyze management's ability to control costs and generate returns for shareholders. Higher profitability often indicates competitive advantages and operational efficiency.

    **Common Ratios**: ROE, ROA, ROIC, Gross/Operating/Net Margins
  </Card>
  <Card title="Financial Health Metrics" icon="shield-check" color="#6b7280">
    **Purpose**: Assess company's balance sheet strength and financial stability

    **Key Insight**: Evaluate ability to meet obligations, manage debt, and maintain operations during economic downturns. Critical for risk assessment and long-term investment viability.

    **Common Ratios**: Debt-to-Equity, Current Ratio, Interest Coverage, Free Cash Flow
  </Card>
  <Card title="Growth Metrics" icon="rocket" color="#6b7280">
    **Purpose**: Quantify how fast a company is expanding its business and revenues

    **Key Insight**: Identify companies with sustainable competitive advantages and market expansion potential. Growth must be balanced with profitability and financial health.

    **Common Ratios**: Revenue Growth, EPS Growth, Book Value Growth, Dividend Growth
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
